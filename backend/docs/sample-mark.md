## kindergarten a – november 2024 (consolidated report)

**school:** euro kids international, berlin campus  
**class:** kindergarten a (ages 4–6)  
**teacher:** ms. anna schmidt  
**period:** november 1–30, 2024  

---

### overall classroom dynamics and emotional regulation

november was an emotionally intense month marked by high energy, excitement, and frequent emotional reactions among students. children showed enthusiasm during group activities and special events but often struggled to regulate emotions during competitive or unstructured situations.

while students could verbally explain classroom rules and identify emotions during guided activities, many were unable to apply these strategies in the moment without adult intervention. conflicts frequently escalated quickly, particularly during free play, transitions, and competitive settings.

adult intervention was required in multiple situations to separate students and restore order. due to time constraints, not all incidents were followed by reflective discussions.

---

### impact of competitive and high-energy activities

a major contributor to heightened emotions was the inter-school sports day held mid-month. most students participated enthusiastically, and the class performed well overall. however, competition also triggered disagreements, physical conflicts, crying, refusal to participate, and frustration related to winning and losing.

similar emotional responses were observed outside of sports day during other high-energy group activities. these patterns suggest that competitive and stimulating events significantly influence student behavior and emotional stability.

---

### safety, supervision, and parent response

several minor health and safety incidents occurred during the month, primarily during active play and sports-related activities. all incidents were handled using standard first-aid procedures, and parents were informed promptly.

parents expressed mixed reactions: pride in participation and performance, alongside concerns about visible conflicts, injuries, and emotional distress. safety procedures such as evacuation drills showed improvement compared to previous months, indicating some positive operational progress despite behavioral challenges.

---

### end-of-month summary

overall, november highlighted the class’s strong engagement and enthusiasm, alongside clear challenges in emotional regulation, conflict management, and behavior during competitive or unstructured situations. these themes represent the dominant patterns observed throughout the month.
